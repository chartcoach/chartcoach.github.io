Labels & filters

Labels are the quickest way to narrow the catalog when you already know the angle you care about.

They work well for things like chart type, task, audience, or communication goal, and they pair nicely with search when you want to move from broad browsing to something more specific.

Label format

Section titled “Label format”

Most labels follow a family:value structure:

  • chart:bar
  • task:compare
  • audience:novice

Some labels are just a single token with no :. These are treated as standalone values.

Filtering behavior

Section titled “Filtering behavior”

On the guidelines listing page:

  • Clicking a label filters the list client-side.
  • You can stack multiple filters (AND semantics).
  • A family filter like chart matches any chart:* label.
  • A full filter like chart:bar matches that exact label.

Good ways to use labels

Section titled “Good ways to use labels”
  • Start broad with a family filter when you want a quick overview.
  • Add a full label when you want to narrow the list to one specific case.
  • Combine labels with site search (⌘K) when you want search to find the general area and labels to tighten the list.

Tip: labels are most useful when many guidelines seem related and you want to focus on one audience, task, or chart family.
